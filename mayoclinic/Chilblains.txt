It's caused by exposure to damp air that's cold but not freezing. Symptoms may show up a few hours after being in the cold.Chilblains can be prevented by limiting your time in the cold, dressing warmly and covering exposed skin. If you do get chilblains, keeping the skin warm and dry can help ease the symptoms.Chilblains, also known as perniosis, usually clears up in 2 or 3 weeks, especially if the weather gets warmer.
