The common cold is an illness affecting your nose and throat. Most often, it's harmless, but it might not feel that way. Germs called viruses cause a common cold.Often, adults may have two or three colds each year. Infants and young children may have colds more often.Most people recover from a common cold in 7 to 10 days. Symptoms might last longer in people who smoke. Most often, you don't need medical care for a common cold. If symptoms don't get better or if they get worse, see your health care provider.Illnesses of the nose and throat caused by germs are called upper respiratory tract infections.Most often, common cold symptoms start 1 to 3 days after someone is exposed to a cold virus.
