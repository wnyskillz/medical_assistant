Atrial tachycardia is a fast heartbeat, called an arrhythmia. It's a type of supraventricular tachycardia (SVT).During an atrial tachycardia episode, the heart beats more than 100 times a minute. Then it returns to a heart rate of around 60 to 80 beats a minute. An episode may start slowly or it may start suddenly and quickly. It can cause a pounding or racing heartbeat, light-headedness, dizziness, and fainting.Atrial tachycardia is common. It may occur in people who have had heart surgery or who are pregnant.
