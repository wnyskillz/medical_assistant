This valve is called the pylorus valve. In pyloric stenosis, the pylorus muscles thicken and become abnormally large, blocking food from reaching the small intestine.Pyloric stenosis can lead to forceful vomiting, dehydration and weight loss. Babies with pyloric stenosis may seem to be hungry all the time.Surgery cures pyloric stenosis.Symptoms of pyloric stenosis usually appear within 3 to 5 weeks after birth.
